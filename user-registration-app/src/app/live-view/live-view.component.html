Hello World: Welcome to Python

Garrett Sullivan

New to programming? Never programmed before? That's ok! This Introduction to Python Workshop is designed for those who have either minimal programming experience or none at all. We will be using the popular programming language, Python, to introduce hackers to the art of making their own basic program. If you want to learn how to code, stop by this workshop to see how fun and easy it can be!

Introduction to UNIX

Eric Pauley

In this Introduction to UNIX workshop, we will be discussing virtual machines and their real world appliations. Hackers will be guided in everything from setting up their own virtual machines, to the basics of Linux, and to important terminal commands. At the end of this workshop, participants will be able to make the most of their newly setup virtual machines!

Introduction to HTML/CSS

Ryan O'Neill

Hi there hacker! New to coding? Little or no experience? That's ok! For those of you interested in learning about coding in HTML with CSS, then you'll definitely want to stop by our Introduction to HTML/CSS Workshop. We'll be going over the basics of HTML and CSS while helping hackers create their own "Hello, World" program!

Databases

Chanpreet Singh

Our Databases Workshop will give you the chance to learn about the basics of databases in SQL. In this workshop, you will be able to learn to create and maintain databases, write SQL queries, and understand the applications of databases.

Interview Prep

B McElroy

This Interview Prep Workshop hosted by Google will focus on getting our hackers ready for an interview! We'll be going over how to prepare your resume, things that companies look for, how to approach an interview, and the differences between a co-op, internship, and a full-time interview.

INTERMEDIATE

Hardware Prototyping

David McDermott

Our Hardware Prototyping Workshop is definitely not to be missed! Learn everything from how to download a basic program onto a Raspberry Pi Board, to the many applications it can be used for. This workshop is very hands on, so come prepared to learn! Hardware will be provided.

Cybersecurity

Trent Jaeger

There are many different types of attacks that exploid your network vulnerabilities. Being aware of these threats and knowing how to prevent/stop them is an invaluable resource to companies and small businesses alike. Stop by our Cybersecurity Workshop for a basic overview on network security and to gain some valuable skills in being proactive about attacks.

Web Development

Angela Chan

Our Web Development Workshop will take the brief overview of the basics of HTML from our previous workshop and expand them to do incredible things. If you want to find out how to design your website and use JavaScript to make a stunning webpage, definitely pay a visit!

Virtual Reality

Adlan Ramly

Come see the members of our Penn State VR Club delve into the possibilities of VR technology at our Virtual Reality Workshop! Starting with a discussion on the development of a VR application, this workshop will talk about the tools and software you need to learn, practice, and implement your own Virtual Reality projects!

App Development/Swift

Zach from Apple

Interested in making your own mobile app? Stop by our App Development Workshop hosted by Apple to gain the basic skills and tools you'll need to begin coding, designing, and implementing your own app with Swift! Whatever your idea may be, this workshop will be a great place to learn the basics and help you get started!

Introduction to Cloud Computing

Pranav Jain

This workshop will be the beginning of a two-part workshop. In this Introduction to Cloud Computing Workshop, hackers will be taught how to write backend systems using Node JS. Additionally, they will be writing REST APIs and learning how to use them to store and retrieve information. This is a unified presentation with the Cloud Computing Workshop, that focuses on backend systems and continuous integration.

Cryptocurrency

Come to our Cryptocurrency Workshop if you are interested in learning more about the basics of cryptocurrencies and blockchain technology. This workshop will give a discussion about what a cryptocurrency really is, how they were developed, and why they're important.

Cloud Computing

Machine Learning

Yuya Ong

What really is the difference between AI and machine learning? Come to our Machine Learning Workshop to find out more about Machine Learning. In this workshop, you will learn how computers can utilize pattern recognition and predict based off of computational statistics to "learn", improve performance, and solve specific tasks by being fed data instead of explicit programming!

How to Take Your Project to Market!

Nick Jacobson

The members of the Mood Triggers team will be talking about the trials and tribulations of taking your product from prototype to distribution!

HackPSU General Judging Criteria Creativity and Originality
- Is the solution novel, original and not-obvious?
- How different is the design from current solutions?
- Does it approach the problem from an unusual or different perspective?
- How creative is the problem statement?
- Does the solution address a problem which is novel, original and not-obvious?

Technical Skills
- Was the solution technically challenging? (Taking into account team size and measured relative to competition)
- How intricate was the solution?
- Did the solution pose a challenge to the group?

Implementation
- Was the solution effective in addressing the problem?
- Was the solution easy to operate for the target demographic? (user friendliness)
- Is the solution needlessly complex?
- Overall, is the solution viable in addressing the problem statement?

Clarity
- Was the team able to convey their ideas in a way understandable to the audience?
- Were the topics accessible to the audience?
- Were all obscure acronyms or jargon explained?

Knowledge
- How effectively did the team handle questions?
- Did the team demonstrate knowledge of the subject matter?
Google Cloud Platform Judging Criteria The best use of Google Cloud Platform's compute, data, storage, or machine learning APIs.
GSK Judging Criteria The product meets deliverables, is creative, and has high clarity. We encourage you to be creative with what already exists, such as in-phone technology, but we still want your idea to be out of the box. Ideal responses will be user-friendly, and explore ways to encourage adherence to the program.

GSK Sleep Tracker Solution First Place: $1,600 We are seeking a simple solution to monitor sleep and wakefulness. This could be a mobile app that uses the microphone in one’s phone to record patterns and/ or monitor sleep and breathing, or by taking a completely different approach to tracking sleep that doesn’t require a phone. Other alternative solutions could involve targeting less tech-savvy patients who might be more included to use something other than a smartphone.

Our goal is to measure all aspects of sleep quality e.g. duration, periods of wakefulness, hours, times waking, episodes of sleepiness during the day, coughing, breathlessness, wheezing etc.
