Creativity & Originality
  1. Is the solution novel, original and not-obvious?
  2. How different is the design from current solutions?
  3. Does it approach the problem from an unusual or different perspective?
  4. How creative is the problem statement?
  5. Does the solution address a problem which is novel, original and not-obvious?
Technical Skills
  1. Was the solution technically challenging? (Taking into account team size and measured relative to competition)
  2. How intricate was the solution?
  3. Did the solution pose a challenge to the group?
Implementation
  1. Was the solution effective in addressing the problem?
  2. Was the solution easy to operate for the target demographic? (user friendliness)
  3. Is the solution needlessly complex?
  4. Overall, is the solution viable in addressing the problem statement?
Clarity
  1. Was the team able to convey their ideas in a way understandable to the audience?
  2. Were the topics accessible to the audience?
  3. Were all obscure acronyms or jargon explained?
Knowledge
  1. How effectively did the team handle questions?
  2. Did the team demonstrate knowledge of the subject matter?

Sleep Tracker Solutionmore_vert
Sleep Tracker Solutionclose

We are seeking a simple solution to monitor sleep and wakefulness. This could be a mobile app that uses the microphone in one's phone to record patterns and/ or monitor sleep and breathing, or by taking a completely different approach to tracking sleep that doesn't require a phone. Other alternative solutions could involve targeting less tech-savvy patients who might be more included to use something other than a smartphone.

Our goal is to measure all aspects of sleep quality e.g. duration, periods of wakefulness, hours, times waking, episodes of sleepiness during the day, coughing, breathlessness, wheezing etc.
