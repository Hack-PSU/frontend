FAQ

Where can I get hardware?

MLH provides hardware for rent free to all hackers! Click here to be taken to the hardware checkout app, and make sure you select our event. From there, you can see a list of hardware, and select what you'd like to rent out. To pick it up, visit the hardware booth (next to registration, look for the banner).
Link: https://hardware.mlh.io/

Can I sleep at the event?

Yes! We have air mattresses for checkout at the registration table starting at 9pm. All we ask is that you treat them nicely and please return them when you're done!

When can I get travel reimbursement?

Travel reimbursements will be handled at the registration table starting Sunday at 10am. Please come to the table with everyone that you traveled with, and the receipts for your transportation costs. If you spoke with us directly about special consideration, please have any emails/messages shared open and ready!

How does extra credit work?

If you are attending and a professor is offering extra credit for being at HackPSU, please refer to the requirements they set out for receiving extra credit. We will be tracking attendance to events like workshops and meals, and will be reporting back to professors with data so that they may confirm you were here.

Do I need to stay at the event?

You are free to come and go as you please to get some rest, fresh air, or take a break! If you feel like throwing in the towel for the weekend, that's fine too! All that we ask is that you only work on your project while on the premises.

Rules
  1. All participants must be older than 18, and must bring a valid form of identification.
  2. Participants must abide by the expectations set in the MLH Code of Conduct, or you will be asked to leave the event.
  3. Teams may be comprised of up to five members.
  4. Projects should be original works created on site. Coming with an idea in mind is perfectly fine, working on an existing project is not.
  5. All submissions must be recieved through DevPost by 2:00PM on Sunday.
  6. Students are permitted to come and go from the venue as they please.
  7. Anything you create is your work, HackPSU and its partners have no claim over intellectual property produced at the event.
Prizes & Challenges

HackPSU Tech Prize

The standard HackPSU experience - work together alone or in a team to build something awesome! We'll have tons of great mentors and other staff around to help you on your way. Choose this track if you're passionate about technology and development.

HackPSU Design Prize

A new way to experience HackPSU - with designers front and center. At the start of the event all challengers will be provided a design prompt. Through mocks, use-flows, and wireframes we will be looking at how your designs best address the prompt.

Design Prompt

Design an application user interface for National Park rangers that will help them manage their park.

Please demonstrate your design through mocks, wireframes, and use flows.

SUBMISSION: To submit please visit our DevPost, link below. All submissions must be received before the hacking deadline (2pm Sunday). This is a hard deadline - submissions received after this time will not be considered for prizes.

You can get a table assigned to you for judging here. Enter your project name, team member emails, and the categories that your project falls under. This is important so that all relevant sponsors are able to judge your project. Only one person from your team should submit the project.

RECOMMENDED: We suggest submitting as early as possible - a submission does not need to contain the final version of your code! All we need to know is your team name, team members, and what challenges you are competing in.

For a complete list of prizes, including sponsor prizes, please visit our DevPost here!
Link: https://hackpsu-fall-2018.devpost.com

Amazon Alexa
Shane Kramer
Location: Auditorium
Amazon Alexaclose

Voice represents the next major disruption in computing: The primary user interface for computing started with character based interfaces, then moving on to GUI, and later to touch screens. These inflection points seem to happen about every 10 years. We are on the cusp of the next one. The rise of the Voice User Interface, or VUI. You can be a part of the voice revolution by creating new capabilities or skills for Alexa, the voice service that powers Amazon Echo and provides capabilities, or skills, that enable customers to interact with devices in a more intuitive way using voice. Examples of skills include the ability to play music, answer general questions, set an alarm or timer, and more. During this workshop you'll learn the basics of how to build skills for Amazon Alexa to run on Alexa devices like the Amazon Tap or Echo. This workshop covers setting up Alexa Skills Kit, developing your first skill, deploying your skill to AWS Lambda, and submitting your skill for publication.

Uses: mlhlocal.host/alexa-code, Amazon Web Services account

KCF Technologies: Coding in Javascript and D3
Zackary Ridall
Location: Workshop Room 3
KCF Technologies: Coding in Javascript and D3close

This workshop is inspired by waiting. Be it at the doctors, the movies, or a bar the average person spends about 6 months of their life waiting in a line. Our task is to take some of the guesswork out of waiting by keeping track of the number of people currently at a location and providing a user with information about the status of the room. This could mean showing the current number of people in the room, the maximum occupancy, how long the wait is to get in, announcements made by the owner, etc. This project will require creating a react interface for the administrator that would allow them to edit statistics about the room and post new events. It also requires a client interface for displaying information to those waiting outside. We have an Arduino starter kit with motion sensors if a team wants the additional challenge of using sensors to monitoring current occupancy automatically.

Uses: An IDE with NodeJS and React support (Recommended: VsCode )
JQuery (Probably not required, but this task will be a lot harder without it)
NodeJS
ReactJS
RECOMMENDED BUT NOT REQUIRED:
NPM (this will be useful for install additional JavaScript libraries if needed)
Arduino Desktop IDE (this can also be used with a web browser through the Arduino Web IDE)
D3 (For displaying graphs, charts, or statistics)
