Rules
  1. All participants must be older than 18, and must bring a valid form of identification.
  2. Participants must abide by the expectations set in the MLH Code of Conduct, or you will be asked to leave the event.
  3. Teams may be comprised of up to five members.
  4. Projects should be original works created on site. Coming with an idea in mind is perfectly fine, working on an existing project is not.
  5. All submissions must be received through DevPost by 5:00PM on Sunday.
  6. Students are permitted to come and go from the venue as they please.
  7. Anything you create is your work, HackPSU and its partners have no claim over intellectual property produced at the event.

Nonvolatile Processors
John Sampson
Location: 124 Business Building (Workshop Room 1)
Time: 8:00 PM - 8:45 PM Saturday
Blockchain with Blockchain@PSUclose

Developments in both software and hardware techniques to leverage on-chip integration of nonvolatile storage elements to provide continuity in the face of frequent power interruptions have rapidly expanded the research area of intermittent computing. Nonvolatile processors, wherein the hardware can provide software-transparent support for intermittency, are a promising design paradigm that strongly synergizes with energy-harvesting and enable novel optimizations from microarchitectural to system levels. In this presentation, I will discuss the challenges inherent in intermittent computing environments, and discuss how work both at Penn State and other institutions has endeavored to expand what types of computations are achievable in such complicated operating conditions.

This workshop has software requirements:

  • 1) Read the workshop materials
  • 2) Download and install node.js
  • 3) Download and install truffle framework
