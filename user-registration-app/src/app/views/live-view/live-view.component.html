Rules
  1. All participants must be older than 18, and must bring a valid form of identification.
  2. Participants must abide by the expectations set in the MLH Code of Conduct, or you will be asked to leave the event.
  3. Teams may be comprised of up to five members.
  4. Projects should be original works created on site. Coming with an idea in mind is perfectly fine, working on an existing project is not.
  5. All submissions must be received through DevPost by 5:00PM on Sunday.
  6. Students are permitted to come and go from the venue as they please.
  7. Anything you create is your work, HackPSU and its partners have no claim over intellectual property produced at the event.

FAQ

Where can I go to get help?

We have a help corner in discord!

Do I need to stay at the event?

Nope! The event is completely virtual this year.

How do I submit a project?

All projects will be submitted through the HackPSU Devpost. Do NOT submit your project via email, Discord, slide into a DM, messenger pigeon, drone, overnight express mail, etc. Both hardware and software projects are allowed. Only one Devpost submission per team is needed. Participants/teams will be asked to upload a 2 minute video showcasing their project as well as questions to answers about the logistics and specifications of your build. View more requirements when submitting a project in the Devpost Rules. Link: https://hackpsu-spring-2021.devpost.com/

How does extra credit work?

If you are attending and a professor is offering extra credit for being at HackPSU, please refer to the requirements they set out for receiving extra credit. We will be tracking attendance to events like workshops, and will be reporting back to professors with data so that they may confirm you were participating.

What’s a Devpost?

Devpost is a project-submitting platform used by many hackathons and technology-focused events. You and/or your team will be asked to submit your project through our Spring 2021 Devpost. For those who have never used Devpost before or would like a refresher, there will be a Devpost overview workshop hosted on Sunday, March 21st from 12:00-1:00PM EST via Zoom.
