ABOUT US

This April 7th, join us and over 600 students from across the nation for 24 hours of creation, innovation, & fun. From seasoned coding veterans to first-time hackers from any major or field, all are welcome to join. Discover your ability to create change by developing technology to solve real-world problems, working with industry leaders, and collaborating with your peers. Of course, we also offer free food, swag, prizes, and much more for your enjoyment!

FAQ

What is a hackathon?
Pretty much the coolest thing ever.
Hackathons foster a sense of community, drive innovation, and build the technical leaders of the future. Here at HackPSU, our goal is to create a collaborative environment where people can both innovate and learn new skills.

Do I need a team?
Not at all! While some people may come with teams ready, many others will not have created teams yet, or may only have partial teams. Before the event begins, we will have a team building workshop for all participants who need teams.

Who can participate?
MLH Hackathons are open to any student currently enrolled in full time higher education. Graduates who graduated less than a year ago may also attend.

What should I bring?
You'll need a laptop, or some kind of device you can program on. You might also want to bring a toothbrush/toiletries. Make sure to have your student ID for check-in!
How long is the hackathon?
The hackathon runs from 12:30pm Saturday to 5:30pm Sunday, with hacking starting at 2:00pm Saturday and concluding 2:00pm Sunday, for a total of 24 hours.

What about food?
Food, drinks, and snacks will all be provided for free! Make sure to let us know of any dietary restrictions when you register.
Can high schoolers come?
Anyone in high school, who is over the age of 18, may participate with a valid ID.
What is the Code of Conduct?
The MLH Code of Conduct ensures that all guests have a positive experience at our hackathon!
How many people can be on a team?
Teams are limited to a maximum of 5 people.
How do I get there?
HackPSU is taking place in the Business building on campus this year. The official address is:

Business Building
University Park, PA 16802

The easiest way to get to State College is either by driving or taking a bus. We will be providing up to $75 in reimbursement for those traveling with 2 or more people, with exceptions made on a case by case basis.

How much does it cost?
$0! The event is completely free to attend!
Is there travel reimbursement?
Yes! We are offering hackers who carpool with 2+ people $60 in reimbursements, and hackers who carpool with 4+ people $75 in reimbursements. We may offer more on a case by case basis.
